Bibimbap

Bibimbap means in Korean mixed rice, all the ingredients are put separately in a stone bowl called Dolsot... It may seem like a small detail, but when you taste it, it changes everything because the bowl, which is heated before being garnished, keeps the ingredients warm and enhances the taste experience. Generally speaking, Korean cuisine is a tasty and elegant cuisine that celebrates diversity, color and balance. Based on rice for energy, fiber (vegetables in equal quantities, such as zucchini, carrots, mushrooms) for vitamins and minerals, an egg or meat for protein, chili or kimchi (pickled cabbage) for taste, you can also add seaweed rich in antioxidants! And to play with flavors and aromas, you can play with a variety of seasonings such as garlic, spring onion, sesame oil or chili paste.

Macaroons

Macaroons were created in Arab countries. They were presented in the form of a cookie in the past. The original recipe of macaroons is composed of almond powder, sugar and egg whites. They appeared in France during the renaissance at the time of the marriage of Catherine de Medicis with the Duke of Orleans. After their appearance in France its original recipe was revisited but it is only in 1830 in Paris that the Parisian pastry cooks decided to assemble them two by two by filling them with a small jam called "ganache". Ceviche

Emblematic dish of Peru it comes in several forms Fresh, refreshing, tangy, ceviche is the first dish I've ever had with raw fish*( not really*). Why not really? This dish is composed of raw fish and various other ingredients but the second main ingredient is lemon (yellow, or green). The acid in the lemon juice chemically denatures the proteins in your fish, in the same way that proteins are denatured when heated. It's a very fine dish that should be appreciated when the good weather comes back.
